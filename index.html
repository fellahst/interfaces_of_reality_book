About the Book

This book explores the fundamental concept of interfaces as the boundaries where different systems, entities, and realities meet and interact. Through the lenses of physics, biology, cognitive science, and technology, we examine how interfaces shape possibilities, enable communication, and define the boundaries of what is real and what is possible.

Reality is not fundamentally made of things, but of stable interfaces navigating a structured space of possibilities. This is not a denial of matter or physics, but a shift in what we treat as primary: from self-contained objects to the constraints and boundaries that allow stable patterns to exist at all.

Once you begin looking at the world through this lens, ideas that had previously seemed unrelated begin to align. Platonic notions of form, category theory's emphasis on transformations, the Free Energy Principle, and artificial intelligence all point toward the same underlying structure once we stop treating things as primary and start paying attention to the conditions that make things possible.
